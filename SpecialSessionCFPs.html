ADMA 2026 Special Sessions

Overview

The ADMA 2026 special sessions provide focused forums for researchers and practitioners to discuss emerging topics, methodological advances, and real-world applications in data mining, artificial intelligence, and advanced analytics. Each special session is organised by domain experts and will follow the same submission, review, and publication standards as the main conference.

Authors are invited to submit original research papers to the relevant special session through the ADMA 2026 CMT submission system. Accepted papers will be included in the ADMA 2026 conference proceedings, subject to the same publication requirements and deadlines as the main track.

Submission Guidelines

Authors should submit their papers through the ADMA 2026 CMT submission system:

Submission site: https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/ADMA2026

When submitting a paper, authors should select the relevant Special Session Track and choose the appropriate special session subject area in CMT.

All submissions must follow the same formatting requirements, page limits, double-blind review policy, and publication policies as the ADMA 2026 main conference submissions.

Each paper will be reviewed by the programme committee of the corresponding special session. Each special session team is responsible for inviting suitable reviewers/PC members and coordinating the review process for papers submitted to their session.

Detailed Version

1. Special Session on Private, Secure, and Trustworthy Data Analytics

Acronym: PSTDA2026

Brief description: This special session focuses on privacy, security, and trust challenges in data analytics. Topics include privacy-preserving technologies, privacy attacks, federated learning, machine unlearning, data poisoning attacks, model evasion attacks, adversarial learning, model robustness, large language model and multi-agent security, secure machine learning with cryptographic techniques, IoT data security, and blockchain techniques for data and model protection. The session invites original research exploring cutting-edge advances in private, secure, and trustworthy data analytics. High-quality accepted papers may be recommended to associated journal special issues.

Organisers:
- Lina Yao, The University of New South Wales, Australia
- Xiaolong Xu, Nanjing University of Information Science and Technology, China
- Deepak Puthal, United Arab Emirates University, UAE
- Haolong Xiang, Nanjing University of Information Science and Technology, China

Website: https://pstda2026.github.io/

2. Educational Data Mining in the Era of Large Language Models

Acronym: EDM-LLM

Brief description: Educational Data Mining has expanded from performance prediction and knowledge tracing to learning analytics, personalised learning, multimodal interaction analysis, and the use of large language models for tutoring, assessment, feedback, and content generation. This special session brings together researchers from data mining, artificial intelligence, and education to examine how advanced mining methods and LLM-based approaches can support scalable, adaptive, and evidence-based learning systems. The session welcomes methodological advances, empirical studies, and practical applications across classroom, online, and lifelong learning contexts.

Organisers:
- Weining Qian, East China Normal University, China
- Yijun Duan, Kyoto Institute of Technology, Japan
- Yun Liu, Kyoto Institute of Technology, Japan

Website: Not applicable

Main contact: Yijun Duan, yijun@kit.ac.jp

3. Advanced Time Series Analysis: Theories, Methods, and Applications

Brief description: This special session focuses on state-of-the-art theories, methods, and applications in advanced time series analysis. Topics include machine learning and deep learning approaches, high-dimensional and multivariate time series analysis, anomaly detection, uncertainty quantification, real-time and streaming data analysis, spatiotemporal data mining, explainable time series models, transfer learning, and domain-specific applications in finance, healthcare, climate science, cybersecurity, and industrial systems.

4. Intelligent Analytics for Healthcare and Biomedicine

Brief description: This special session focuses on intelligent analytics, data-driven methods, and advanced computational techniques for healthcare and biomedicine. It aims to bring together researchers and practitioners to discuss novel methodologies, intelligent healthcare systems, and real-world applications that leverage healthcare, biomedical, and population health data to improve clinical decision-making, personalised medicine, disease prevention, and healthcare delivery.

Organiser:

5. 2nd Special Session on Adaptive and Trustworthy Knowledge Discovery

Brief description: The rapid expansion of interconnected data ecosystems poses new challenges in knowledge discovery, requiring both adaptability and trustworthiness. As data environments grow in complexity, reliable and robust discovery processes are increasingly important. This special session aims to bring together researchers, practitioners, and industry experts to explore recent advances and challenges in adaptive and trustworthy knowledge discovery, with applications across finance, healthcare, cybersecurity, industrial automation, and other dynamic decision-making domains.

Organisers:

6. Data-Efficient Agentic Learning for Data Mining

Acronym: DEAL-DM

Brief description: This special session focuses on data-efficient agentic learning for data mining. It addresses intelligent systems that can perceive data or environments, reason and plan, retrieve and reuse knowledge, interact with tools or users, and improve from feedback under realistic constraints such as limited labelled data, costly feedback, noisy environments, and high-risk decision making. Topics include agentic data mining, autonomous data analysis workflows, experience augmentation, memory and retrieval-augmented agents, planning and tool use, in-context adaptation, budget-efficient reinforcement learning, human-in-the-loop mining, personalisation with limited data, scientific discovery, healthcare, decision support, robustness, safety, privacy, and trustworthy evaluation.
